Research Focus

I am a Lead Algorithm Engineer working at the intersection of 3D Computer Vision, Computer Graphics, and Deep Learning.

My work focuses on recovering and generating 3D models, including mesh reconstruction, material reflectance estimation (BRDF), and generative 3D Modeling.

I am particularly interested in differentiable rendering pipelines and large-scale synthetic data generation for training deep learning networks in 3D understanding tasks.

Selected Projects

Light Field - Depth Estimation
Developed deep learning algorithms for depth estimation using light field camera
3D Reconstruction
Depth-guided 3D Asset Generation: Depth → mesh reconstruction → textured 3D asset
Flexigon ↗
Flexigon: Text-Guided 3D Mesh Editing and Generation - a Blender-based framework for text-guided 3D mesh deformation and topology-aware geometry generation, combining geometry processing with generative methods.
Multi-bounce Reflectometry ↗
Developed a differentiable rendering framework for physically-based rendering (PBR) targeting estimating the Bidirectional Reflectance Distribution Function (BRDF) for material recovery for real-world objects. The approach extends to concave geometries and leverages the modeling of multi-bounce light transport in complex scenes.
Synthetic Data Generation
Pipeline for generating large-scale synthetic datasets with controllable assets and domain randomization, spanning diverse human, indoor, and outdoor scenes, and a broad distribution of material properties ranging from specular and diffuse to opaque, transparent, and mixed reflectance behaviors.
